Our Evolutionary Timeline

How localized volunteer grassroots actions matured into an internationally acclaimed, registered trust.[span_1](start_span)[span_1](end_span)

2000
Year 2000

The Pioneer Support Spark

Founder Tariro Chikwanha officially launches the baseline community project within Chitungwiza by systematically organizing initial community support groups for individuals and families directly living with HIV.

2006-2008
Years 2006 - 2008

Formal Institutional Legal Trust

To scale health and youth response interventions across regional districts, the organization formalizes its operating protocols, culminating in the official approval of **Trust Registration Number MA457/2008**[span_2](start_span)[span_2](end_span).

Recognition Milestone

Diplomatic PEPFAR Hero Award

The U.S. President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief (PEPFAR) and the United States Embassy in Zimbabwe officially honor Tariro Chikwanha with a certificate of recognition, celebrating her sustained commitment and courage in the regional HIV response.

Honoring a Local Hero & Community Pioneer

Known across Chitungwiza for launching foundational health safety nets, Tariro Chikwanha pioneered the region's earliest dedicated community greenhouse projects designed specifically to promote sustainable living. Today, she continues to spearhead urban smart farming systems to secure youth livelihoods and food safety vectors.
